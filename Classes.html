Fall Term Classes

Our fall classes span from September 4th to October 23rd. Here is an overview of what will be covered for fall:

Week 1: Navigation

Learn the fundamentals of navigating without a phone, tricks for never losing camp and a map and compass scavenger hunt.

Week 2: Fall foraging

Learn 5 different wild edibles and their uses in the fall. Walk, learn, forage and taste testing.

Week 3: Fall fire craft

Learn the fundamentals of creating a fire in the fall, gathering tinder, kindling and fuel. Overview of 3 different fire lays, fire starting methods and fire triangle. We will also cover fire safety and how to properly extinguish a fire.

Week 4: Primitive Water Filtration

Learn how to find water in the fall, how to filter and purify water and the importance of water in a survival situation. Use of primitive filtration methos to create drinking water with nothing more than a knife and a container.

Week 5: Primitive Cordage

Learn to make cordage from natural materials with simple techniques and tools. Cover the uses and best plants to use for fibers.

Week 6: Lashing and Knots

Learn the fundamentals of lashing and knots in a survival context. Cover different types of knots, their uses and applications in building shelters and creating tools.

Week 7: Traps

Learn how to build primitive traps for hunting and fishing. Cover different types of traps, their uses and how to set them effectively.

Week 8: Shelter Building

Learn how to build different types of shelters using natural materials. Cover the principles of shelter building, site selection and how to create a warm and dry shelter in the fall.
