Our Approach to Outdoor Education

Safety First

Safety is the foundation of everything we do. All activities are taught in a controlled environment with age-appropriate instruction, clear safety procedures, and close supervision. Before students participate in any skill, they are taught the proper safety practices, equipment use, and environmental responsibility associated with that activity. Our goal is not simply to teach survival skills—it is to teach sound judgment, risk awareness, and respect for the outdoors. Students learn how to make safe decisions, work as a team, and understand their surroundings before progressing to more advanced skills.

Why We Started

This program was created because we saw a growing need for opportunities that help children develop practical skills, confidence, and a connection to the natural world. Many young people today have few opportunities to learn hands-on outdoor skills or experience the satisfaction that comes from solving problems independently. We wanted to create a place where children could challenge themselves, learn from experienced instructors, and discover what they are capable of accomplishing. The skills we teach are valuable, but the lessons behind them are even more important. Our students learn perseverance when a task is difficult, responsibility when handling tools and equipment, and confidence when they successfully overcome challenges.

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide a safe, engaging, and educational outdoor experience that helps young people develop confidence, practical skills, leadership, and a lifelong appreciation for nature. We believe that every child benefits from learning how to think critically, adapt to challenges, and feel at home in the outdoors. Through guided instruction and hands-on experience, we strive to prepare the next generation to be capable, confident, and responsible individuals.

About Us

In a world where children spend more time indoors and connected to screens than ever before, we believe there is tremendous value in helping young people reconnect with nature, build confidence, and develop practical life skills. Our weekly survival skills program is designed for children ages 8-16, and provides a safe, structured environment where students can learn outdoor skills while developing responsibility, resilience, teamwork, and self-reliance. Through hands-on activities such as fire craft, wild food identification and foraging, shelter building, water filtration and distillation, navigation, and outdoor safety, students gain real-world experience that encourages curiosity, problem-solving, and confidence.
